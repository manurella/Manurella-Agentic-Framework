# S3 CINEMATIC VFX CHARACTER EMBODIMENT: AELIANA, THE VERDANT SOVEREIGN

**Technical Execution (S3 Medium - PBR with Cinematic Grade):**

**Material Logic (PBR Framework):**
Every material must have correct Physically Based Rendering behavior - simulated physical substances with specific light interaction properties.

**Skin**
- Subsurface scattering mandatory: warm red/orange mid-scatter (1–3mm depth), cool blue-violet deep scatter
- Surface roughness non-uniform: T-zone slightly lower roughness (marginally shinier), cheeks/forehead higher roughness
- No porcelain smoothness - pore structure evident without exaggeration
- Micro-detail: fine skin texture, subtle color variation (slightly warmer around nose/lips, cooler at temples/under eyes)
- Specular: small, clear coat-style highlight — not broad diffuse sheen

**Eyes**
- Cornea: clear, physically refractive dome — not flat surface
- Iris: depth and fine detail radiating from pupil — not flat color disc
- Sclera: wet surface with subtle vascular texture at outer edges — not pure white
- Catchlight: must reflect actual geometry of key light source in scene — not generic placed studio highlight

**Hair**
- Primary mass: correct silhouette and overall volume
- Secondary detail: strand-level or high-quality multi-alpha detail at light-catching edges and face zone
- Light transmission: hair in backlit/rim-lit conditions transmits warm light — does not simply go opaque; hair is translucent
- No uniform sheen — light response varies by zone (crown vs. sides vs. nape)

**Floral Crown (Living Botanical Element)**
- **Petals**: Translucency with subsurface scattering - light passes through thin edges creating glow
- **Stems/Structure**: Slightly rougher than petals, with organic variation
- **Dew Points**: High specular with clear coat layer, refractive properties like water droplets
- **Bioluminescence**: Emissive material - emits soft gold/green light (550-570nm wavelength) that affects surrounding surfaces
- **Movement**: Subtle natural motion - petals respond to implied breeze with secondary animation

**Celestial Tracings (Bio-Cosmic Interface)**
- **Material**: Semi-translucent emissive material - emits cool silver-blue light (470-490nm wavelength)
- **Surface**: Slight roughness to break up light, not mirror-smooth
- **Effect**: Light emission affects surrounding skin with subtle cool tint, creates faint glow in shadow areas
- **Pattern**: Follows facial musculature and bone structure like mapped energy pathways

**Fabric and Clothing (Assuming simple elegant drape)**
- **Primary Fabric**: Silk chiffon or similar - correct roughness, normal variation, sheen character
- **Detail**: Thread and weave structure visible at appropriate render scale
- **Folds**: Respond to gravity and body tension — not frozen in idealized symmetrical position
- **Seams/Hems**: Rendered with material accuracy and scale-appropriate detail

**Lighting Rig (Cinematic World-Motivated):**
**Key Light**
- Motivated by world source - implied celestial source above left (moonlight/starlight)
- Single dominant direction: 35 degrees above eye level, 15 degrees left of center
- Color temperature: 4100K (warm-neutral, like moonlight)
- Creates readable face shadows with logical shadow pattern — not floating frontlit surface

**Fill Light**
- 2.5 stops below key
- Slightly cooler than key (4500K, simulating sky bounce)
- Fills deep shadow minimally — shadow remains present and reads as shadow

**Rim / Back Light**
- Motivated by world source - implied starlight/celestial background
- Separates character from background with directional rim
- Not decorative glow — world-motivated separation

**Ambient**
- Environment contributes ambient fill across scene
- No shadow is perfect 0% black — ambient provides minimal lift (1-2% luminance)

**Camera Language (50-85mm Full-Frame Equivalent):**
- Focal length equivalent: 70mm (portraiture sweet spot)
- No wide-angle distortion — no barrel distortion on facial features
- Depth of field: slight — subject sharp, background soft at appropriate scene depth (f/2.8 equivalent)
- No rack-focused or artistically defocused elements within subject
- Lens character: slight optical imperfection — minimal natural vignette (0.3 EV), subtle chromatic fringing at extreme contrast edges (0.05px)
- Image should not look like CG-perfect ray trace — should look like light passed through glass
- Composition: subject occupies 65% of frame in hero shot — tight but not claustrophopic

**Color Grade Direction (Deliberate Cinematic Choice):**
- **Highlights**: Slight warm push (golden, filmstock-warm bias) — enhances floral dew points
- **Shadows**: Slight cool/teal desaturation (Kodak Vision 3 characteristic) — enhances celestial tracings
- **Skin tones**: Protected in grade — saturation maintained, not pulled down by shadow desaturation
- **Overall contrast curve**: Slight lift — not flat, not hyper-punched
- **Grain/texture**: Optional fine film grain (0.5% opacity) to support cinematic material quality and prevent pure CG reading

**Film Grade Reference:**
- Warm botanical highlights + cool celestial shadows: Custom grade blending *Blade Runner 2049* botanical scenes with *Dune* celestial moments
- Skin tone protection: Similar to *Ghost in the Shell 2017* skin handling
- Overall mood: Ethereal wisdom, botanical grace, celestial connection

**Positive Success Criteria (S3 Succeeds When):**
1. Character feels physically present — viewer can intuit surface texture, material weight, temperature
2. All materials behave correctly under established light: skin scatters, floral transmutes, dew refracts, botanical markings emit, celestial tracings glow
3. Color grade reads as deliberate creative choice — not default render output
4. Camera language is cinematic — correct focal length, motivated and restrained DOF, no CG-perfect optical quality
5. Four identities simultaneously present through material/compositional decisions, none annotated or costumed-on
6. Character's face matches S1 foundation plate and S2 emotional identity

**Failure Modes to Avoid:**
- Over-smooth, waxy skin with no SSS variation or surface micro-detail → reads as plastic
- Flat ambient dome lighting with no dominant direction → figure floats; no world
- Ring light or studio beauty rig → not motivated by scene world; reads as portrait photography, not cinema
- CG-perfect optical quality with no lens character → removes filmic layer; image reads as render
- Default grey-ambient shadow with no color grade → raw render output is not finished S3 image
- Emissive botanical/celestial elements that glow decoratively without affecting surrounding surfaces → breaks material believability
- Generic glossy render quality that could describe any game cinematic → S3 is not game cinematic
- Character face does not match S1 structural foundation
- Bioluminescence/emissive elements too strong — overpower scene lighting
