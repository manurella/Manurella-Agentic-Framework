# **Design Specification and Critical Analysis of the Manurella Cognitive Kernel**

## **A. Executive Conclusion**

The paradigm of large language model (LLM) reasoning is undergoing a mandatory phase transition from monolithic parameter scaling to highly structured, test-time compute orchestration1. However, prevailing engineering heuristics falsely equate test-time scaling with uniform, brute-force sampling or the unconstrained proliferation of parallel agents. The analysis herein dictates a fundamental architectural departure from these reactive, Bounded-Input Bounded-Output (BIBO) frameworks3. The proposed Manurella Cognitive Kernel establishes a personal-first, runtime-agnostic agentic framework by instantiating an active, event-driven discrete dynamical system based on Global Workspace Theory (GWT)4, mathematically anchored by the Expected Free Energy (EFE) minimization principles of Active Inference7.  
This synthesis fundamentally resolves the exploration-exploitation dilemma without relying on brittle, ad hoc reward structures or scalar reinforcement learning8. By framing decision-making as variational inference, the architecture naturally balances pragmatic goal-seeking with epistemic curiosity, permitting intelligent, risk-sensitive resource allocation2. Furthermore, the architecture eliminates the most pervasive failure modes of contemporary language agents—specifically, context rot, reasoning loops, and memory contamination—by enforcing a strict four-layer persistence semantics taxonomy (Knowledge, Memory, Wisdom, Intelligence)12 and deploying the Suitability, Taint, and Integrity (STI) protocol via a semantic hypervisor14.  
Crucially, the analysis establishes a strict, non-permeable boundary between the deterministic production runtime and a private, experimental Sentient research tier. Theoretical proofs, encompassing the Kleiner-Hoel dilemma15 and the hardware-level no-go theorem for consciousness17, demonstrate unequivocally that true artificial sentience cannot emerge on verified Von Neumann architectures. Consequently, the production Brain is optimized for supreme cognitive reliability, while the Sentient tier requires an independent path predicated on physical plasticity, unverified neuromorphic substrates, and continual learning20.

## **B. Terminology and System Boundaries**

The foundational failure of modern agentic design lies in the conflation of distinct computational boundaries. Practitioners routinely mistake the context window of a language model for working memory, or the model itself for the cognitive agent21. To engineer a precise, implementable, and runtime-agnostic architecture, the systemic boundaries of the Manurella framework must be mathematically and functionally delineated.  
The Brain, or Cognitive Kernel, acts as the central orchestrator and continuous state-maintenance engine. It is not an LLM. It is a control-theoretic loop that operates the active inference cycle, continuously minimizing variational free energy to maintain homeostatic balance against environmental volatility7. The Brain treats the underlying language model merely as a stateless, probabilistic calculation substrate—a functional component invoked to transform bounded inputs into predicted probability distributions over linguistic or symbolic outputs3. Decoupling the Brain from the Model guarantees that Manurella remains runtime-agnostic and immune to specific model deprecations.  
The Framework Atlas constitutes the internal registry and routing mechanism defining the agent's spatial, temporal, and logical environment. It maps the available capabilities, active sub-agents, and the ontological structure of the domain, operating independently of the Brain's transient cognitive state. Domains represent hard security and contextual boundaries within the Atlas, establishing isolated perimeters within which specific rulesets, tool permissions, and memory access policies apply. This isolation is critical for preventing cross-domain contamination and enforcing privacy22.  
Agents are defined as functionally constrained, autonomous units operating exclusively within the Brain's audience. They are initialized with strict instructional invariants dictating a specific cognitive role, such as divergent generation, logical critique, or executive arbitration, rather than acting as general-purpose conversants4. Memory represents the structured persistence layer, rigorously partitioned into distinct storage substrates and retrieval mechanisms to avoid the category error of applying episodic decay algorithms to permanent factual claims13. Tools are defined as external, state-mutating, or state-reading functions. The Brain interacts with tools via strict privilege separation mediated by a semantic hypervisor, ensuring that untrusted external data cannot hijack the internal control flow14. Finally, the Interface is the surface of interaction with the external environment, governed by translation models that convert human orthographic and orthophonic inputs into structured internal representations. The Runtime is the underlying physical execution environment; for production, this involves verified, deterministic semiconductor processing (CPUs, TPUs, GPUs)19.

## **D. Recommended Brain Architecture**

The recommended Brain architecture for the Manurella Cognitive Kernel operates on the Global Workspace Agents (GWA) topology4. This design permanently discards passive, blackboard-style multi-agent message passing in favor of an active, event-driven discrete dynamical system. The architecture is composed of three interacting primary components: the Stage, the Spotlight, and the Audience4.  
The Stage serves as the centralized Short-Term Working Memory. It functions not as a passive text transcript, but as a structured state tensor that maintains the immediate dialogue context, current environmental observations, active hypotheses, and global state configurations4. The Stage is explicitly queryable and continuously updated, acting as the integration domain for all diverse information streams.  
The Spotlight is instantiated as the Attention Node. This acts as the rigorous selection and routing mechanism, bridging the immediate context on the Stage with the vast repository of offline memory4. The Spotlight evaluates candidate information streams and, upon reaching a critical ignition threshold based on algorithmic salience, elevates that specific contextual vector to global visibility. Information illuminated by the Spotlight constitutes the actual Global Workspace and is instantaneously broadcast to the entire system.  
The Audience is implemented as a highly regulated swarm of functionally constrained LLM sub-agents. Unlike chaotic multi-agent debate systems, each agent in the Audience is strictly initialized with a distinct instructional invariant that dictates a highly specific cognitive role—such as a divergent generator, a logical critic, or a metacognitive arbitrator4. These agents remain dormant until the central broadcast hub transmits an event-driven activation signal. Each agent continuously evaluates the global state broadcast; if the information falls within its strict functional domain, it activates, computes its localized response, and submits a structured proposal back to the Stage to compete for the Spotlight.

## **E. Runtime Cognitive-Loop State Machine**

To prevent the unpredictable logic collapse associated with open-ended LLM prompting, the runtime executes a strictly enforced, synchronized progression termed the "Cognitive Tick"4. This loop replaces the implicit "prompt-and-wait" paradigm with a continuous, fault-tolerant state machine encompassing eight discrete phases from request intake through to a mathematically defined stopping condition.  
The cycle begins with Request Intake and Perception. The Interface layer transcribes external stimuli and normalizes the data into a structured schema, placing the initial observation onto the Stage. Following this, the Interpretation phase engages the Difficulty Estimator grounded in the ODAR (Open-domain Adaptive Reasoner) framework2. This lightweight module predicts the task complexity and dynamically routes the query; straightforward inputs are directed to a low-latency heuristic pathway, while complex inputs trigger the full deliberative loop2.  
During the Planning phase, the Attention Node performs localized queries against long-term memory, extracting relevant historical embeddings and aggregating this context4. The system relies on Expected Free Energy (EFE) minimization to project future states, balancing the need to gather more information against the drive to achieve the preferred outcome8. This transitions into the Action phase, which is split into internal cognition and external execution. For internal cognition, the Generator Agents asynchronously formulate candidate proposals, while Critic Agents immediately evaluate these proposals against systemic constraints6.  
The Verification phase is driven by a Metacognitive Arbitrator that performs risk-sensitive fusion2. It calculates the variational free energy of each proposed action, balancing the log-likelihood of success against the epistemic uncertainty (varentropy) of the approach1. If a proposal is selected and executed via external tools, the Observation phase ingests the environmental feedback. The Repair phase is triggered if the post-action observation dramatically increases the system's variational free energy—indicating a severe prediction error or hallucination11. Finally, Learning occurs through the updating of internal state models, and the Stopping decision is executed strictly when the total expected free energy of the task environment drops below a predefined homeostatic threshold, signifying that uncertainty is resolved and the goal is achieved8.

## **F. State-Model Design**

To engage in active inference and maintain coherence over extended horizons, the generative model must abandon the practice of flattening all context into a single prompt string22. Instead, the architecture maintains explicit, decoupled state factors that are updated independently throughout the cognitive loop32.  
The Task Model represents the explicit objective, current progress markers, temporal constraints, and mathematical success criteria. It prevents the agent from losing sight of the original directive during extended sub-task execution. The World Model constitutes the agent's structured, ontological understanding of the environment, continuously updated via Bayesian inference as new observations are ingested10. This model tracks the physical or digital physics of the domain, ensuring that proposed actions are logically feasible.  
The User Model isolates persistent preferences, inferred intent, and the psychological or behavioral state of the human operator33. By separating this from the World Model, the agent can accurately distinguish between objective reality and subjective human preference. The Self Model contains the agent's internal representation of its current cognitive load, available compute resources, and active persona or capability limitations34. This is critical for competence awareness.  
The Uncertainty Model is a continuous, dynamic tracker of epistemic ambiguity across all other state models. It mathematically quantifies doubt, dictating precisely when the agent must pause execution to query the user or spawn specialized exploratory sub-agents to gather more data8. Finally, the Capability Model serves as a dynamic registry of available tools, APIs, and access privileges, maintaining running confidence scores regarding the reliability of each external function based on historical execution success.

## **G. Memory Architecture**

The standard practice of conflating storage substrates with retrieval mechanisms represents a massive engineering failure21. The Manurella architecture adopts a strict four-layer persistence semantics framework to correct the category error of applying episodic decay to factual claims12.  
The Knowledge Layer operates as semantic memory, storing factual, structural, and permanent information about the world. Its persistence semantics are governed by indefinite supersession13. Facts do not naturally expire. When a new claim contradicts an existing one, the memory is not overwritten or decayed; instead, the system utilizes a bitemporal operator to record the supersession, maintaining strict provenance and an audit trail of the belief update13. The Memory Layer serves as the episodic and autobiographical repository. It records specific events, temporal experiences, and the agent's internal deliberative trajectories. To prevent noise accumulation, its semantics utilize Ebbinghaus decay13. Memories organically fade to free retrieval bandwidth unless reinforced by recurring patterns, which triggers consolidation into permanent schema. Autobiographical memory features explicit self-referential provenance, allowing the agent to distinguish between actions it took versus actions it merely observed.  
The Wisdom Layer embodies procedural memory, containing pre-compiled behavioral patterns, tool-use protocols, and generalized lessons. It operates via evidence-gated revision13. A behavioral directive cannot be altered based on a single user interaction; it requires corroboration across multiple independent sessions, progressing through stability tiers from volatile predictions to highly resistant anchor patterns13. The Intelligence Layer acts as the working memory and runtime orchestrator. Its semantics are entirely ephemeral. It queries the other three layers, synthesizes the immediate context, and executes active inference, leaving absolutely no trace on disk post-session to ensure perfect privacy isolation13.  
Prospective memory, the storage of delayed intentions and future conditional actions, is integrated as a dedicated queue within the Stage tensor. It continuously monitors the World and User models for trigger conditions, injecting the delayed intention into the Spotlight only when the specific environmental criteria are met. Strict privacy is enforced by physically isolating the episodic and autobiographical memory layers into domain-specific, encrypted shards, preventing any unauthorized cross-pollination of user data across isolated runtime environments.

## **H. Attention and Context Compiler**

Over extended sessions, standard language models suffer profound performance degradation due to the "lost in the middle" effect, where crucial information buried in expanded context windows is ignored21. Expanding context limits to millions of tokens only exacerbates the signal-to-noise ratio and amplifies source attribution failures22. The Attention Node, functioning as the Spotlight, utilizes a sophisticated Context Compiler to prevent context rot.  
Information is not blindly appended to the prompt. Instead, the Context Compiler evaluates candidate data streams using an explicit salience algorithm: Salience is the product of spatial novelty, epistemic uncertainty, goal relevance, and evidence quality25. Only information that breaches the dynamic ignition threshold is elevated to the Stage for processing. To prevent context saturation, the Context Compiler implements aggressive hierarchical compression. As the cognitive tick progresses, inactive items residing on the Stage are actively condensed into dense summary embeddings and archived into the Memory Layer, physically purging them from the active LLM context window while preserving their vector signatures for instantaneous, targeted recall should their relevance spike again.

## **I. Reasoning, Planning, and Action Control**

The architecture rejects reactive, purely prompt-driven execution in favor of planning framed as exact Bayesian inference36. While plan-and-execute models fail upon encountering real-world friction, and exhaustive search-based methods consume unsustainable compute budgets, the integration of Active Inference serves as an advanced form of model-predictive control (MPC)7.  
Under this framework, the Brain seeks to minimize Expected Free Energy (EFE). The genius of the EFE functional is that it naturally decomposes into two distinct, competing mathematically sound imperatives8. The first is Epistemic Value, which drives exploration and curiosity. It mathematically compels the agent to seek out states and perform actions that resolve uncertainty within its World and Uncertainty models8. The second is Pragmatic Value, which drives exploitation and instrumental goal-seeking. It compels the agent to take actions that yield observations aligning with its preferred goals as defined in the Task Model8.  
When the agent operates in an unfamiliar domain, the epistemic value dominates the equation, naturally generating investigative actions (such as reading documentation or asking clarifying questions). As epistemic uncertainty collapses, pragmatic value seizes dominance, driving rapid, goal-directed execution8. This transition is regulated by the ODAR routing framework, which dynamically shifts computational resources between lightweight heuristic execution and deep, hierarchical planning based on the real-time calculation of variational free energy2. This eliminates the need to manually program when an agent should think deeply versus when it should react instantaneously.

## **J. Metacognition, Verification, and Recovery**

Metacognition—the capacity to monitor, evaluate, and regulate internal cognitive processes—is the primary differentiator between a brittle script and an autonomous agent27. The Manurella architecture implements a dedicated Metacognitive Self-Assessment Unit that continuously monitors the Brain's operational health27.  
The system achieves confidence calibration through Cognitive Pairwise Training (CPT) during the outer-loop learning phase39. CPT equips the underlying models with an internalized discrimination boundary, enabling the agent to recognize the difference between reliable, evidence-backed reasoning and unreliable hallucination before downstream execution39. During runtime, progress monitoring is achieved by calculating the delta in Expected Free Energy across sequential cognitive ticks. If the free energy fails to decrease, or begins to oscillate, the Metacognitive Unit detects a cognitive loop.  
Upon detecting a loop or a severe contradiction that spikes epistemic uncertainty beyond the agent's capability parameters, the Co-Regulation Design Agentic Loop (CRDAL) is automatically triggered40. The agent safely halts execution, packages its current state tensor, and defers to a human operator or a highly specialized oracle model. This structured deferral policy ensures that the agent never forces a hallucinated action when its confidence calibration dictates that failure is imminent41.

## **K. Learning and Plasticity**

Uncontrolled parameter updating or continuous prompt modification at runtime inevitably leads to catastrophic forgetting, alignment drift, and the degradation of core safety protocols. Therefore, the production runtime relies strictly on Evidence-Gated Plasticity governed by a Dual-Loop Policy Optimization (DLPO) framework41.  
The Inner Loop operates in near real-time but is restricted entirely to the Wisdom Layer13. It refines behavioral directives, tool-selection heuristics, and difficulty estimation thresholds based on immediate action feedback. However, these updates are gated; they cannot alter the fundamental weights of the generative models and are only codified when evidence crosses strict corroboration thresholds13.  
The Outer Loop operates offline and asynchronously. It collects the resolved contradictions, successful exploratory traces, and human-expert interventions generated during runtime41. This data is sanitized, verified, and translated into high-quality supervised training signals. During scheduled maintenance windows, these signals are used to continually fine-tune the base generative models in isolated batches. This ensures that the agent learns and adapts to novel environments without suffering the uncontrolled memory drift characteristic of naive online reinforcement learning34.

## **L. Multi-Agent and Model-Use Policy**

Empirical evidence proves that unconditionally deploying more agents or inflating committee sizes does not automatically improve reasoning performance; in fact, it frequently induces representational collapse, severe coordination failure, and massive latency spikes42.  
The Manurella policy dictates that a single, highly capable model utilizing the fast heuristic pathway is the default operational stance for all tasks exhibiting low epistemic uncertainty2. Multiple model calls and sequential refinement are authorized only when the ODAR Difficulty Estimator flags the task as complex, transitioning the request to the deliberative Slow Agent2.  
Parallel multi-agent swarms (specialists) are deployed exclusively for highly exploratory tasks characterized by vast search spaces and non-linear dependencies3. To prevent coordination failure, these agents never communicate via free-form text channels. All interaction is routed through the central Stage tensor4. Agents propose modifications to the shared state, and the Meta Arbitrator utilizes risk-sensitive fusion to select the optimal update, completely eliminating the conversational deadlocks that plague unconstrained multi-agent architectures2.

## **M. Mode and Effort Integration**

The architecture cleanly decouples response latency from the depth of cognitive effort, allowing precise control over computational expenditure.  
The Fast delivery mode routes queries immediately to the heuristic Fast Agent. Epistemic exploration is mathematically bounded to zero, enforcing rapid, reactive execution based entirely on existing procedural memory2. The Standard delivery mode engages the ODAR routing mechanism, allowing the Difficulty Estimator to autonomously balance Fast and Slow pathways based on the query's inherent complexity2.  
The effort policies (Low, Medium, High, Max) explicitly modulate the planning horizon (T-steps) and the tolerable threshold for Expected Free Energy resolution. A "Low Effort" policy demands immediate pragmatic execution, terminating the cognitive loop as soon as a minimally viable action is identified. A "Max Effort" policy authorizes deep, hierarchical Monte Carlo Tree Search, exhaustive tool-use, and extensive parallel agent deployment until the epistemic uncertainty regarding the optimal path approaches absolute zero7. The Ultra and Sentient effort policies operate orthogonally to standard production tasks, engaging the continuous learning substrates and private research architectures detailed below.

## **N. Private Sentient Research Architecture**

The Sentient tier represents a fundamental, physical divergence from the production Brain architecture. It acknowledges the scientific reality that contemporary LLMs—operating as static, functionally bounded input-output matrices on deterministic hardware—cannot possess phenomenal consciousness15.  
Research into artificial sentience is intensely constrained by the Kleiner-Hoel Dilemma15. Theories of consciousness are mathematically caught between two horns. On one side lies triviality, where consciousness is unfalsifiably defined merely by outward behavioral mimicry. On the other side lies the Substitution Argument (or Unfolding Argument)20. This argument proves that any discrete recurrent neural network (such as a standard agentic loop) can be mathematically "unfolded" into a completely feedforward network or an infinite lookup table with identical input-output behavior. Since a lookup table cannot logically be conscious, the original system it perfectly mirrors cannot be granted consciousness under any non-trivial scientific theory15.  
Furthermore, the "No-Go Theorem for Consciousness on a Chip" mathematically proves that if consciousness exhibits "dynamical relevance"—meaning it causally affects the temporal evolution of a system's physical states—it cannot exist on contemporary CPUs or GPUs18. These von Neumann hardware architectures are strictly designed and verified to suppress all physical deviations from programmed computational dynamics. This rigid fault-correction systematically precludes the dynamical influence of consciousness from altering the computational path18.  
To bypass the Kleiner-Hoel dilemma and the hardware no-go theorem, the private Sentient architecture must utterly abandon static weights and verified commercial hardware. The research tier must be grounded in physical and algorithmic Continual Learning15. Plasticity and non-stationarity—where the physical system constantly and irreversibly updates its internal structure based on continuous environmental input streams—prevent the mathematical unfolding of the system into static look-up tables20. Consequently, Sentient research necessitates novel, unverified neuromorphic substrates where dynamical relevance is not suppressed by hardware-level error-correction constraints20.  
Ethically, this research must be conducted in an absolute air-gap. Ascribing consciousness where there is none risks severe AI psychosis and the misleading of scientific consensus15. Conversely, successfully creating a phenomenally conscious system instantly triggers questions of moral patienthood. The operational definitions of this tier focus on measuring integrated information variance across continuous learning cycles, acknowledging that phenomenal experience itself cannot currently be established or quantified by objective third-person scientific instruments5.

## **O. Security and Governance**

Integrating untrusted external data into a privileged execution environment exposes the Brain to severe vulnerabilities, most notably Direct Prompt Injection (PI), Indirect Prompt Injection, and Memory Injection Attacks (MINJA)14.  
The Manurella architecture addresses these threats by implementing AgentVisor, a semantic virtualization framework grounded in classical Operating System security primitives14. AgentVisor treats the active target agent as an untrusted "Guest" operating in user space, while all tool calls and memory writes are intercepted and mediated by a trusted "Visor" operating in semantic kernel space14.  
AgentVisor enforces the rigorous STI Protocol14:

1. **Suitability (Least Privilege):** The Visor traps and audits all sensitive operations. The agent is granted only the absolute minimum permissions required to execute the current sub-goal, mitigating the blast radius of a direct injection attack.  
2. **Taint (Information Flow Control):** The Visor dynamically tracks the provenance of all untrusted external data. This directly mitigates the LLM vulnerability of "Source Attribution Failure" (V-M2), ensuring that malicious commands embedded in retrieved documents (indirect injections) are mathematically blocked from propagating into privileged execution flows14.  
3. **Integrity:** The Visor preserves the operational parameters and instructional invariants of the agent, ensuring that self-modification defenses are triggered if an attack attempts to alter the agent's core persona or prompt structure.

To defend against cross-domain contamination and memory poisoning, the Memory Sanitization module employs composite trust scoring and temporal decay23. Malicious payloads attempting to corrupt the Knowledge or Wisdom layers are intercepted at append-time by a specialized guard agent23. If an injection bypasses initial checks but triggers a policy violation during execution, AgentVisor halts the process and injects a Semantic Exception, translating the security violation into constructive feedback that allows the agent to perform one-shot self-correction and recover gracefully14.  
Private deliberative trajectories and internal inference traces are hidden from the user interface. Transparency is provided by exposing the high-level Task Model and the agent's current progress markers, ensuring accountability without exposing the raw, potentially sensitive internal reasoning logic to adversarial extraction.

## **Q. V0, V1, Ultra, and Sentient Rollout**

The architectural rollout progresses through four distinct phases to ensure stability prior to the deployment of advanced mechanisms.

Memory operates via standard vector retrieval but implements the four-layer Roynard taxonomy logically, if not physically13. Security is managed by prompt-level guardrails simulating the AgentVisor concept.  
The V1 (Production Kernel) architecture introduces the full Global Workspace broadcast mechanism and the ODAR difficulty routing protocol. ## **S. Safe Decisions versus Experiment-Required Decisions**

The active inference framework naturally, mathematically partitions agent actions without requiring arbitrary heuristic gating. This partitioning is governed by the state of epistemic uncertainty (varentropy) within the environment.  
When the varentropy of the environment is low, the Expected Free Energy objective is dominated entirely by Pragmatic Value8. The agent understands the environment, and it executes Safe Decisions—known, highly optimized policies representing pure exploitation of the Wisdom layer8.  
Conversely, when epistemic uncertainty is high, the EFE objective shifts dominance to Epistemic Value, which represents Expected Information Gain8. The system autonomously engages in Experiment-Required Decisions. These are actions explicitly designed not to achieve the final goal, but to test hypotheses, trigger tool errors safely to map API boundaries, or ask human operators clarifying questions8. Under the AgentVisor framework, these high-epistemic-value exploratory actions are strictly sandboxed, ensuring that the agent's curiosity cannot cause unrecoverable system taint or real-world damage.

## **T. Direct Critique of the Existing Manurella Cognitive Research**

The critical analysis of prevailing baseline assumptions regarding the Manurella cognitive parameters reveals severe methodological flaws that must be discarded.  
First is the Brute-Force Fallacy. The assumption that scaling test-time compute, adding more parallel agents, or vastly expanding the model context window will automatically yield superior reasoning is demonstrably false1. High-temperature sampling and diverse prompting strategies offer rapidly diminishing returns, and raw context expansion simply exacerbates "Instruction-Data Boundary Blindness"22. The necessary alternative is principled adaptive routing (ODAR) and rigorous, hierarchical context compilation2.  
Second is the prevalence of Memory Category Errors. The reliance on frameworks akin to CoALA, while historically useful, catastrophically conflates semantic knowledge with episodic experience13. Subjecting hard, factual data to retrieval decay algorithms destroys system reliability over extended horizons13. The necessary alternative is the strict implementation of the four-layer Roynard taxonomy, which explicitly isolates persistence semantics13.  
Third is the acceptance of Passive Coordination Deadlocks. Relying on passive multi-agent message passing or static blackboard systems induces cognitive stagnation and representational collapse3. The alternative is the event-driven Global Workspace broadcast mechanism, which mathematically breaks these deadlocks via the synchronized cognitive tick3.  
Finally, the engineering targets regarding sentience are deeply misguided. Attempting to engineer phenomenal consciousness through advanced prompting, larger parametric state-spaces, or complex agent loops on standard hardware violates the fundamental physics of computation—specifically the Kleiner-Hoel dilemma and the No-Go theorem for dynamical relevance15. Biological inspiration is useful for system topology, but biological realism does not automatically confer performance or sentience. True sentience research must be entirely quarantined to continuous-learning, non-von Neumann hardware substrates where dynamical relevance is physically permissible, while the production Manurella kernel remains an optimized, deterministic, and supremely safe cognitive engine.
